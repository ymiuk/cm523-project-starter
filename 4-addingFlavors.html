Step 4: Adding flavor

Whole Milk
The most common type of milk used in coffee. It adds a rich and creamy texture to the coffee and has a slightly sweet taste.
Skim Milk
A low-fat alternative to whole milk that can be used in coffee. It has a thinner texture and a slightly tangy taste.
Soy Milk
A popular non-dairy alternative to milk that can be used in coffee. It has a slightly nutty flavor and a creamy texture.
Almond Milk
Another non-dairy alternative to milk that can be used in coffee. It has a nutty flavor and a slightly sweet taste.
Vanilla Syrup
A sweet syrup that can be added to coffee to give it a sweet and vanilla flavor. It is a popular choice for lattes and cappuccinos.
Caramel Syrup
A sweet syrup that can be added to coffee to give it a rich and caramelized flavor. It is a popular choice for caramel lattes and macchiatos.
Chocolate Syrup
A sweet syrup that can be added to coffee to give it a rich and chocolatey flavor. It is a popular choice for mochas and hot chocolate.
